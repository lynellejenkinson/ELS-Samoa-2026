01

Introduction & Acknowledgements

This manual describes a systematic approach for initiating the emergency management of patients with serious illnesses, providing an outline of the basic skills needed.

This manual does not cover trauma management specifically, although many principles apply.

02

Summary of Standardised Approach

Core principle: Simultaneous assessment and treatment of abnormalities as they are found.

Step 1 — Triage

CategoryAction
EMERGENCYImmediate — call for help
PRIORITYRapid — front of queue
NON-URGENTCan wait their turn

Step 2 — Initial stabilisation

  • Position — appropriate to clinical situation
  • Airway — patent and protected; C-spine precautions if needed
  • Breathing — rate and effort; assist if inadequate; O2 if cyanosed
  • Circulation — if no cardiac output: BLS immediately; IV access; 10–20 mL/kg Normal Saline if shocked
  • Disability — GCS/AVPU; pupils; temp; fingerprick BSL; treat hypoglycaemia
  • Monitor and Reassess — P, BP, RR, GCS continuously

Steps 3–5

  • Directed history and examination
  • Commence specific treatment
  • Ongoing care and patient handover
03

Initial Stabilisation

Treat each abnormality before moving to the next step. Restart the sequence if the patient deteriorates at any time.

Positioning

  • Unconscious — supine for active treatment; recovery position otherwise
  • Dyspnoeic — upright
  • Child / partial airway obstruction — sitting on parent's lap
  • Shock — supine
  • Head injury — head elevated 30 degrees
  • Third trimester pregnancy — left lateral or wedge under right flank

Airway

TypeSigns
Complete obstructionNo air movement; paradoxical breathing; cyanosis
Partial obstructionStridor; self-posturing; wheezing; weak cough
Potential obstructionNormal air entry but burns or swelling to airway
  • Suction — secretions, blood, vomit
  • Head tilt (avoid if C-spine injury), then chin lift, then jaw thrust
  • Airway devices — OPA (Guedel), NPA, LMA
  • Foreign body — back blows and abdominal thrusts

Breathing

  • Assess rate, effort, symmetry, SpO2; listen to chest
  • Assist if inadequate — BVM or mouth-to-mask; give oxygen if cyanosed
  • Tension pneumothorax — needle thoracostomy (2nd ICS, MCL) then chest tube

Circulation

  • No cardiac output — BLS immediately (30:2)
  • Cardiac output present — PR, BP, capillary refill; IV cannula
  • Shock — 10–20 mL/kg Normal Saline; cardiac monitor; correct arrhythmias

Disability

  • GCS 8 or less, or AVPU = P or U — airway at risk
  • Pupils — size, symmetry, reaction
  • Temp + fingerprick BSL — hypoglycaemia — 0.25–1 g/kg IV dextrose
Monitor and Reassess: P, BP, RR, GCS continuously. Restart ABCDE if patient deteriorates.

05

Other Emergency Skills

Femoral vein cannulation

When peripheral access is unavailable. Femoral vein lies medial to the femoral artery in the femoral triangle. Use Seldinger technique. Confirm by aspiration of dark venous blood.

Intercostal catheter (chest tube)

For pneumothorax (after needle decompression), haemothorax, large pleural effusion. Site: 4th–5th ICS, mid-axillary line, over top of rib. Connect to underwater sealed drain.

Always perform needle thoracostomy first in tension pneumothorax before formal chest tube insertion.

Needle cricothyroidotomy

Rescue for cannot intubate, cannot oxygenate. Insert 14G cannula through cricothyroid membrane at 45 degrees caudally. Confirm with air aspiration. Connect to 15 L/min O2 via Y-connector, 1 second on, 4 seconds off. Buys approximately 30–45 minutes.

Bridge only — a definitive surgical airway must follow promptly.

Bronchodilator administration

Salbutamol 2.5–5 mg nebulised in 3 mL Normal Saline at 6–8 L/min O2. Repeat every 20 min. Add ipratropium 0.5 mg. IV salbutamol 250 mcg slowly if not responding.

Needle thoracostomy

14–16G cannula, 2nd ICS mid-clavicular line, above 3rd rib. Hiss of escaping air confirms tension pneumothorax. Leave cannula until chest drain inserted.

Appendices

Glasgow Coma Scale

DomainResponseScore
EyesSpontaneous / To voice / To pain / None4/3/2/1
VerbalOriented / Confused / Words / Sounds / None5/4/3/2/1
MotorObeys / Localises / Withdraws / Flexion / Extension / None6/5/4/3/2/1

GCS 8 or less = airway at risk. Maximum 15, minimum 3.

AVPU scale

  • Alert — Voice — Pain — Unresponsive

BLS key points

  • Rate 100–120/min, depth 5–6 cm; allow full recoil
  • 30 compressions : 2 ventilations
  • Attach AED as soon as available; minimise interruptions

Paediatric key points

  • Weight (kg) estimate: (Age + 4) x 2
  • Fluid bolus: 10 mL/kg; reassess after each bolus
  • Compression:ventilation — 15:2 (two-person CPR)
  • Defibrillation: 4 J/kg  |  Adrenaline: 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO

Oxygen cylinder duration

Duration (min) = Pressure (kPa) x Cylinder factor ÷ Flow rate (L/min). D ≈ 0.16 | E ≈ 0.28 | F ≈ 0.98
